# 1. Verification of AI answers

## 1. Source citation (Mandatory referencing)
* **Rule** - Every critical fact, statistics or historical claim must be backed by a verifiable source or publication.
*  **Prompt example:**
>"Create a 10-bullet-point test of the main causes of World War 1 for high school students. **Crucial:** For every signle fact or date you mention, state a specific historical source, textbook standart, or official educational website where this information can be verified."

## 2. Setting guardrails for Educational Content
AI tends to please the user, which often leads to the making up "facts". You must set strict boundaries and define the AI's role as a responsible educator before generating materials.
### Recommended guidelines:
1. **Define the Role:** Tell the AI to act as an experienced textbook author or pedagogy expert.
2. **The Honesty rule:** Explicitly tell the AI that saying "I don't know" is better than guessing.
* **Example prompt:**
> "Act as a expierinced high school teacher and textbook editor. I need you to create a short reading comprehension text about math functions. **Rule:** Stick strictly to established scientific facts. If you are unsure about any detail or if the data is scientifically debated, do not include it. It is better to write a short text than to include unverified information."

## 3. Cross-checking: Feeding output into a second AI
### Option A: The manual Workflow
1. **Step 1:** Use **AI tool  #1** (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini) to generate content (e.g, math quiz)
2. **Step 2:** Copy the generated quiz.
3. **Step 3:** Open **AI tool #2** (e.g., Claude, Microsoft Copilot) in a new tab and you can use this exact peer-review prompt:
> "I am a school teacher. The text below was generated by another AI for my classroom. Act as an exprent pedagogical auditor. Review the material, list any factual innaccuracies or misleading questions, and provide a corrected final version."
