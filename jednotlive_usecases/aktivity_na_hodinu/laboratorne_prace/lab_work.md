# AI-Powered Lab Work & Experiments: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Enhance hands-on science education by using AI to generate experiment protocols, safety checklists, and observation sheets. This guide helps science teachers design safe, engaging, and curriculum-aligned laboratory activities while ensuring students focus on critical thinking and data analysis.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **[ChatGPT](../../../jednotlive_tools/chatboti/chatgpt/chatgpt.md) / Claude:** Excellent for drafting step-by-step procedures and predicting potential safety hazards.
* **[Gemini](../../../jednotlive_tools/generacia_obrazkov/gemini/gemini.md):** Useful for researching modern alternative materials for traditional experiments (e.g., household substitutes).
* **Canva:** For formatting clean, professional-looking lab manuals and observation templates.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Select Concept] ➔ [Draft Procedure] ➔ [Generate Safety Checklists] ➔ [Create Data Tables] ➔ [Review]`

### Step 1: Select the Scientific Concept
Identify the core principle students should observe (e.g., osmosis, chemical reactions, or laws of motion).

### Step 2: Draft the Experiment Procedure
Ask the AI to generate a step-by-step guide. Specify the available equipment to ensure the experiment is practical for your classroom.

### Step 3: Generate Safety Protocols
Command the AI to identify potential risks (e.g., "using heat," "handling acids") and provide clear safety instructions for students.

### Step 4: Create Observation & Data Sheets
Use AI to design structured tables where students can record their findings, measurements, and reflections.

## ✍️ Effective Prompting

### Prompt Template:
> "Act as a Science Laboratory specialist. I want to design an experiment for [Grade Level] students about [Scientific Concept] using [Available Materials]. Provide a step-by-step 'Student Procedure', a 'Safety Checklist', and a 'Data Recording Table'."

### Example Prompt:
> "Act as a Chemistry teacher. Design an experiment for 8th graders about pH levels using red cabbage juice as an indicator. Available materials: Red cabbage, vinegar, baking soda, clear cups, and water. Provide clear student steps, safety rules, and a table to record color changes."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Safety First:** **MANDATORY:** Always physically test any AI-generated experiment yourself before allowing students to perform it. AI may miss subtle safety nuances.
* **Scientific Accuracy:** Verify that the predicted outcomes and chemical equations are 100% correct according to scientific standards.
* **Equipment Match:** Double-check that all materials suggested by the AI are actually available in your school lab.
* **Privacy Warning:** Do not share student lab results or personal identifiers in the prompt.

## 🚀 Optional Extension: "What If" Scenarios
Ask the AI to generate a list of "Troubleshooting Questions" (e.g., "What happens if the color doesn't change? Why might this occur?") to help students think critically when an experiment doesn't go as planned.
