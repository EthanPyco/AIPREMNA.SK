# AI-Powered Flashcards: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Reduce the hours spent manually designing study materials by using AI to instantly generate vocabulary lists, definition cards, and question-and-answer decks tailored to any topic or age group. This guide helps teachers create effective, engaging, and personalized flashcards for students to support active recall.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **Gemini / ChatGPT / Claude:** Excellent for generating large sets of terms, definitions, and example sentences.
* **Quizlet / Anki:** Digital platforms where you can import AI-generated content (e.g., via CSV) for students to use.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Select Topic/Content] ➔ [Draft Prompt] ➔ [Generate Cards] ➔ [Format for Import] ➔ [Review]`

### Step 1: Select Your Topic or Source
Paste existing lesson notes, a textbook paragraph, or simply specify a subject and grade level to provide context for the AI.

### Step 2: Draft Your Prompt
Define the exact structure you need (e.g., "Front: [Term], Back: [Definition]"). Specify the target audience to ensure appropriate reading levels.

### Step 3: Format and Import
Ask the AI to output the data in a format compatible with your chosen tool (e.g., "Format as a table" or "Format as a comma-separated list for CSV").

---

## ✍️ Prompt Engineering Section

### Prompt Template:
> "Create a list of [Number] flashcards for [Grade Level] students about [Topic]. Format the output as a clear list with 'Front: [Term/Question]' and 'Back: [Definition/Answer]'. Include [Optional: Contextual Examples/Hints]."

### Example Prompt:
> "Create a list of 15 flashcards for 6th-grade biology students about the human digestive system. Format the output as a clear list with 'Front: [Question]' and 'Back: [Simple Answer]'. Ensure the definitions are concise and appropriate for 11-year-olds."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Accuracy:** Verify that every term matches the textbook or curriculum definition.
* **Conciseness:** Ensure the "Back" of the card is short enough for quick mental recall.
* **Privacy:** **Mandatory Warning:** Do not include individual student names or sensitive personal data when generating flashcard sets.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Generate a set of at least 10 flashcards from a single textbook paragraph.
* Format AI output for bulk-uploading into a digital tool like Quizlet or Anki.
* Differentiate a flashcard deck by creating a "Simplified" version for students who need more support.
* Verify that the generated definitions are pedagogically sound and factually correct.
