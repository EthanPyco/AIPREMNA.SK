# AI-Powered Socratic Dialogue & Oral Exam Prep: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Scale critical thinking and oral exam preparation by using AI as a Socratic debate partner. This guide helps teachers set up AI-driven simulations where students must defend their arguments, uncover logical fallacies, and deepen their understanding through interactive intellectual sparring.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **ChatGPT / Claude:** Ideal for complex, multi-turn dialogues and maintaining a specific persona.
* **Custom GPTs:** Allows teachers to pre-configure a "Socratic Tutor" with specific behavioral guardrails.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Select Topic] ➔ [Define AI Persona] ➔ [Interactive Debate] ➔ [Analyze & Reflect]`

### Step 1: Choose a Debatable Concept
Select a topic from your curriculum that has multiple perspectives (e.g., historical decisions, ethical dilemmas in science, or literary interpretations).

### Step 2: Configure the "Socratic Bot"
Define the AI's role (e.g., "Strict Judge," "Skeptical Scientist") and set strict rules: the AI must never give answers and never agree immediately.

### Step 3: The Intellectual Sparring Session
Students engage with the AI for a set number of rounds. The goal is for the student to survive the AI's critiques and provide evidence-based defenses for their claims.

### Step 4: Transcript Review
Students download their chat logs to analyze where the AI caught them using weak logic or where they successfully defended a difficult point.

---

## ✍️ Prompt Engineering Section

### Prompt Template:
> "Act as a [Persona] in a debate about [Topic] for [Grade Level].
> 
> **Behavioral Rules:**
> 1. Use the Socratic method to challenge the student's claims.
> 2. Never agree with the student on the first response.
> 3. Never give direct answers; instead, ask a challenging follow-up question.
> 4. Keep responses under 3 sentences."

### Example Prompt:
> "Act as a strict but fair opponent in a debate about renewable energy for a 9th-grade physics class. The student will present a claim. Your job is to find logical fallacies in their argument, question their sources, and make them defend their position. End every response with a question."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Fact Check:** Remind students that AI can "hallucinate" fake statistics; they should challenge the AI if they suspect a bluff.
* **Frustration Check:** Ensure the AI's intensity matches the students' maturity level to keep them motivated.
* **Pedagogical Alignment:** Monitor whether the debate stays on the core learning objective or wanders into tangents.
* **Privacy Warning:** **MANDATORY:** Never share sensitive student data during these sessions. Use anonymized profiles if necessary.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Configure an AI prompt that enforces a Socratic teaching style (no direct answers).
* Design a debate persona tailored to a specific historical or scientific topic.
* Implement a "Pre-writing Sandbox" where students must defend their thesis to an AI before writing an essay.
* Guide students in analyzing AI transcripts to identify logical fallacies.

## 🚀 Optional Extensions
* **Historical Roleplay:** Ask the AI to "Argue strictly as an anti-federalist during the US Constitutional debates."
* **Fallacy Hunting:** Have students highlight the exact moment in the transcript where the AI used a "strawman" or "circular reasoning" argument.
