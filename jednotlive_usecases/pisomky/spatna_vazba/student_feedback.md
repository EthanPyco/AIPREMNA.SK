# AI-Powered Personalized Student Feedback: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Provide every student with high-quality, actionable, and growth-oriented feedback in seconds. This guide helps teachers use AI to analyze student work (essays, projects, or short answers) and generate personalized comments that highlight strengths and provide clear steps for improvement.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **[ChatGPT](../../../jednotlive_tools/chatboti/chatgpt/chatgpt.md) / Claude:** Best for processing student text and generating nuanced, empathetic feedback.
* **Mote:** A voice-to-text tool that can be enhanced with AI to provide structured audio feedback for students.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Define Rubric] ➔ [Anonymize Work] ➔ [Generate Feedback] ➔ [Refine Tone] ➔ [Final Review]`

### Step 1: Define Your Assessment Criteria
Provide the AI with the specific rubric or "success criteria" used for the assignment. This ensures the feedback is grounded in the learning objectives.

### Step 2: Input Anonymized Student Work
Paste the student's text or a summary of their performance. **Crucial:** Remove the student's name and any sensitive personal identifiers.

### Step 3: Generate Feedback
Ask the AI to provide feedback in a specific format, such as "Two Stars and a Wish" (two things done well, one thing to improve).

### Step 4: Refine for Tone and Encouragement
Ensure the feedback sounds supportive and matches the student's age and maturity level.

---

## ✍️ Effective Prompting

### Prompt Template:
> "Act as a supportive [Subject] teacher. Based on this rubric: [Insert Rubric], provide personalized feedback for a student who submitted this work: [Insert Anonymized Work]. Use a [Desired Format: e.g., constructive/encouraging] tone. Highlight 2 strengths and 1 specific area for growth."

### Example Prompt:
> "Act as a supportive English teacher. Based on my rubric for persuasive writing (focusing on thesis clarity and evidence), provide feedback for a student essay about climate change. Anonymized work: [Insert Essay Text]. Use the 'Two Stars and a Wish' format and ensure the tone is motivating for a 9th grader."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Privacy Check:** **MANDATORY:** Have you removed all names and personal IDs? Never share identifiable student data with an AI.
* **Fairness:** Does the feedback accurately reflect the student's effort and the rubric? Ensure the AI hasn't missed any specific context you know as their teacher.
* **Actionability:** Is the "Wish" (improvement step) specific enough that the student knows exactly what to do next?
* **Tone Match:** Ensure the feedback sounds like *you* and fits the relationship you have with your students.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Use AI to generate feedback that is strictly aligned with a specific grading rubric.
* Format feedback into a constructive "Two Stars and a Wish" structure.
* Anonymize student work effectively before processing it through an AI tool.

---

## 🚀 Optional Extension: Feedback for Revision
Ask the AI to provide "Next Step" prompts (e.g., "Ask the student: How would your argument change if you considered [Perspective]?") to encourage further critical thinking rather than just providing a grade.
