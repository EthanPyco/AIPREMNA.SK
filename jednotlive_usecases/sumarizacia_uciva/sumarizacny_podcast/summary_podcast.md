# AI-Powered Podcast Summaries: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
This guide helps teachers quickly turn long educational podcasts and audio recordings into concise summaries, study guides, and activities. It saves hours of listening time while making expert content accessible for students.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **[NotebookLM](../../../jednotlive_tools/ucebne_pomocky/notebook_lm/notebook_lm.md):** Excellent for uploading audio files directly and generating deep-dive summaries.
* **Whisper (by OpenAI):** Industry-standard for accurate audio-to-text transcription.
* **[ChatGPT](../../../jednotlive_tools/chatboti/chatgpt/chatgpt.md)/Claude:** For refining transcripts into structured study materials.
* **YouTube Transcript Feature:** For quickly grabbing text from educational videos.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Audio/Link] ➔ [Transcription] ➔ [AI Summarization] ➔ [Activity Generation] ➔ [Final Review]`

### Step 1: Preparation
Find a relevant educational podcast or video and obtain its transcript or audio file.

### Step 2: Conversion
Use a tool like Whisper or YouTube's built-in transcript feature to get the raw text.

### Step 3: Summarization
Feed the text into an AI with a specific prompt to extract key points, facts, and themes.

### Step 4: Refinement
Use the AI to generate student-facing materials like worksheets, discussion questions, or cheat sheets.

---

## ✍️ Effective Prompting

### Prompt Template:
> "I am a [Subject] teacher. Here is a transcript from a podcast about [Topic]. Summarize it for [Grade Level] students. Include [Key Components: e.g., 5 bullet points, 3 vocabulary words, 1-paragraph summary]. Tone: [Educational/Engaging]."

### Example Prompt:
> "I am a History teacher. Here is a transcript from a podcast about World War I. Summarize it for 9th-grade students. Provide a 3-sentence overview, followed by 5 key takeaways, and list 3 vocabulary words with definitions. Style: Academic but accessible."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Accuracy:** Verify dates, names, and historical facts against the original audio.
* **Content Filtering:** Remove ads, sponsor messages, or irrelevant tangents.
* **Tone:** Ensure the language level is appropriate for the target grade.
* **Privacy:** Do not share sensitive student data or private school recordings without permission.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Convert an audio file or YouTube link into a text transcript.
* Generate a structured summary with key takeaways and vocabulary.
* Create at least two types of student activities (e.g., discussion questions, fill-in-the-blank) from the summary.
