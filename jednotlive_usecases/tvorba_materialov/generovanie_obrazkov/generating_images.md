# AI-Powered Image Generation: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
This guide helps teachers create high-quality, custom visuals, diagrams, and illustrations for their lessons. It eliminates the need for hours of searching for the "perfect" copyright-free image, allowing for perfectly tailored educational aids.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **DALL-E 3 (via [ChatGPT](../../../jednotlive_tools/chatboti/chatgpt/chatgpt.md)):** Great for following complex instructions and adding simple text within images.
* **Midjourney:** Best for high-artistic quality and realistic textures.
* **Canva Magic Media:** Integrated directly into the popular design tool for seamless classroom use.
* **Adobe Firefly:** Excellent for high-quality, safe imagery with intuitive editing features.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Subject/Concept] ➔ [Style Selection] ➔ [Prompt Refinement] ➔ [Generation] ➔ [Accuracy Review]`

### Step 1: Subject Definition
Identify exactly what needs to be visualized (e.g., a plant cell, a historical scene, or a character from a book).

### Step 2: Style Selection
Choose a visual style that matches the pedagogical goal (e.g., flat vector, 3D render, watercolor, or realistic photo).

### Step 3: Composition & Lighting
Define the camera angle (close-up, wide-angle) and lighting (bright, moody, high-contrast) to set the right tone.

### Step 4: Generation & Refinement
Run the prompt and use follow-up instructions to adjust specific details (e.g., "Make the nucleus bigger" or "Change the background to blue").

---

## ✍️ Effective Prompting

### Prompt Template:
> "[Subject] in a [Style] style. [Composition/Lighting details]. [Background details]. [Color Palette]."

### Example Prompt:
> "A detailed scientific diagram of a plant cell for a 7th-grade biology class. Flat vector style, clean white background, clear labels for nucleus, mitochondria, and cell wall. High resolution, bright colors."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Scientific/Technical Accuracy:** Does the diagram have the correct parts, proportions, and factual details?
* **Text Labels:** Carefully check for spelling errors or "gibberish" text often generated within AI images.
* **Inclusion & Diversity:** Ensure visual representations are inclusive and free from harmful stereotypes.
* **Privacy Warning:** Never use student names or personal photos in prompts to generate lookalike characters or scenes.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Generate a custom icon set for a classroom worksheet.
* Create a scientifically accurate (after review) diagram for a specific lesson topic.
* Adjust an image's style (e.g., from "realistic" to "sketched") using prompt modifiers.
