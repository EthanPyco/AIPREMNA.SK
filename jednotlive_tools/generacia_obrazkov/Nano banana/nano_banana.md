# Nano Banana (Google AI Studio): Standardized Guide for Teachers

---

## 1. General Use Case
Nano Banana, via Google AI Studio, provides teachers with a powerful environment to generate high-quality educational visuals using Google's latest image models (like Imagen). It is designed for creating custom classroom illustrations, diagrams, and creative art that can make complex concepts more visually engaging for students.

### 🛠️ Recommended Use Cases
* **Creating Visual Aids:** Generating specific historical scenes or scientific diagrams that are hard to find in standard textbooks.
* **Creative Writing Prompts:** Producing unique, surreal, or hyper-realistic images to serve as "story starters" for students.
* **Custom Classroom Decor:** Designing posters or icons that match the specific theme or aesthetic of a current unit of study.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step UI Workflow

`[Access/Login] ➔ [Model Selection] ➔ [Prompting/Input] ➔ [Interacting/Refining] ➔ [Exporting]`

### Step 1: Accessing the Tool
Navigate to aistudio.google.com and sign in with your Google account. Ensure you are in the "Creative" or "Multimodal" workspace where image generation models (like Imagen 3) are active.

### Step 2: Providing Input
In the main prompt box, describe the image you want to create. Be as descriptive as possible regarding subject, style (e.g., "watercolor," "3D render"), lighting, and composition. Since this is a developer-focused tool, you can often adjust "Temperature" or "Safety settings" in the right-hand sidebar.

### Step 3: Refining the Result
Review the generated images. If they aren't quite right, add "Negative Prompts" (things you don't want) or adjust your description to be more specific. You can regenerate multiple variations to find the perfect fit for your lesson.

---

## ✍️ Effective Prompting for Nano Banana

Google's creative models respond exceptionally well to artistic styles and technical photography terms.

### How to Write Prompts for this Tool:
* **Define the Medium:** Start with the style, such as "Oil painting of..." or "Isometric 3D icon for..."
* **Lighting and Atmosphere:** Use descriptive words like "soft morning light," "cinematic lighting," or "vibrant neon colors."
* **Subject Details:** Specify the perspective, such as "close-up macro shot" or "wide-angle panoramic view."

### Example Prompt:
> "Create a 3D isometric illustration of a plant cell. Labels should be clear and colorful. Style: Clean, educational, and modern. Lighting: Bright studio lighting with a soft blue background."

---

## ⚠️ Tool-Specific Review Checklist (Shortcomings)

* **Text in Images:** Like many image generators, Nano Banana may struggle with generating complex or long text within the image. Always check labels for spelling.
* **Anatomical Accuracy:** For biological or technical diagrams, verify that the AI hasn't added extra limbs or misplaced parts.
* **Prompt Sensitivity:** Small changes in the prompt can lead to drastically different results. Keep your successful prompts saved.
* **Privacy Warning:** Do not include student photos or identifiable information in the generation prompt.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Mastered This Tool
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Successfully generate a set of three distinct visuals for a specific lesson topic.
* Use style-based keywords to change the aesthetic of an image (e.g., from "cartoon" to "realistic").
* Adjust the aspect ratio or resolution settings to fit a specific document or slide deck.

## 🚀 Optional Extensions
* **Advanced Parameter Tuning:** Use the sidebar to adjust the "Safety filter" if you are generating abstract concepts that are being over-blocked.
* **API Key Generation:** Generate an API key if you want to integrate Nano Banana's image generation into a custom school website or application.
* **Multimodal Prompts:** Upload an existing sketch and ask the AI to "render this into a professional digital illustration."
