# AI & Academic Integrity: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Navigate the complex landscape of student AI usage with confidence. This guide helps teachers transition from "policing" AI to building a culture of transparency and academic integrity, focusing on how to adapt assignments and handle suspected AI misuse.

### 🛠️ Key Concepts
*   **AI Disclosure:** Students clearly stating how and where they used AI.
*   **Process over Product:** Shifting assessment focus to the journey of learning, not just the final result.
*   **Detection Limitations:** Understanding that AI detectors often produce false positives and are not definitive proof of cheating.

---

## 🔄 The Integrity Framework

`[Set Clear Policies] ➔ [Adapt Assessments] ➔ [Educate Students] ➔ [Handle Suspected Misuse]`

### Step 1: Set Clear Policies
Define three levels of AI usage for every assignment:
1.  **NO AI:** Strictly human work (e.g., in-class essays).
2.  **AI AS ASSISTANT:** Allowed for brainstorming or grammar checks, but must be disclosed.
3.  **AI INTEGRATED:** Full use of AI to generate and then critically analyze content.

### Step 2: Adapt Assessments
Move away from "take-home essays" as the primary grade. Incorporate:
*   In-class oral exams or presentations.
*   Reflective journals detailing the writing process.
*   Assignments requiring local/current context that AI cannot easily replicate.

### Step 3: Educate Students
Teach students *why* shortcuts hurt their learning. Discuss "Cognitive Offloading" and the importance of developing their own voice.

## ✍️ Effective Prompting for Teachers

### Prompt Template (Drafting an AI Policy):
> "I am a [Subject] teacher for [Grade Level]. Create a clear and encouraging 'AI Usage Policy' for my syllabus. It should define when AI is allowed, how students should cite it, and why original thinking is important for this subject."

### Example Prompt:
> "I am a 10th-grade History teacher. Create a student-friendly AI policy for our project on the French Revolution. Allow AI for brainstorming but require a 'Reflection Log' showing how the student verified the AI's facts."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
*   **Avoid "Detection Traps":** Never use an AI detector score as the sole evidence for disciplinary action. They are often biased against non-native speakers.
*   **Conversation over Accusation:** If you suspect a student misused AI, start with a curious conversation: "Can you explain your reasoning for this specific paragraph?"
*   **Pedagogical Alignment:** Ensure your policies support the ultimate goal: student mastery of the subject matter.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
*   Draft a clear AI policy for a specific assignment.
*   Identify at least three ways to adapt a traditional assignment to be "AI-resistant."
*   Explain the risks of relying solely on AI detection software.

---

## 🚀 Optional Extension: The "AI-Human Hybrid" Rubric
Create a rubric that specifically grades the *quality of a student's critical analysis* of an AI-generated draft, rather than the draft itself.
