# Claude: Standardized Guide for Teachers

---

## 1. General Use Case
Claude is an advanced AI assistant developed by Anthropic, recognized for its natural-sounding language, high emotional intelligence, and ability to handle very long documents. For teachers, it is the ideal partner for drafting sensitive communications, summarizing extensive pedagogical research, and creating nuanced lesson content.

### 🛠️ Recommended Use Cases
* **[Writing Emails](../../../jednotlive_usecases/administrativa/pisanie_emailu/writing_email.md):** Crafting empathetic and professional messages, especially for sensitive parent-teacher interactions.
* **[Summarizing Notes](../../../jednotlive_usecases/sumarizacia_uciva/sumarizacia_poznamok/summarizing_notes.md):** Extracting insights from long educational PDFs, textbooks, or meeting transcripts.

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step UI Workflow

`[Access/Login] ➔ [Prompting/Input] ➔ [Interacting/Refining] ➔ [Finalizing/Exporting]`

### Step 1: Accessing the Tool
Go to claude.ai and sign in with your email or Google account. Claude's interface is clean and distraction-free, focusing primarily on the conversation.

### Step 2: Providing Input
Type your instructions in the chat box. You can upload multiple files (up to 30MB each) using the paperclip icon. Claude is excellent at "reading" entire chapters to help you plan lessons based strictly on that text.

### Step 3: Refining the Result
Use follow-up messages to adjust the output. A unique feature is "Artifacts," which opens a side window for code, snippets, or structured documents, allowing you to view and edit content alongside the chat.

## ✍️ Effective Prompting for Claude

Claude responds best to detailed context and natural, conversational instructions.

### How to Write Prompts for this Tool:
* **Speak Naturally:** Claude understands complex nuances better than most models; you don't need to use overly technical "AI speak."
* **Provide Examples:** If you want a specific style of worksheet, upload a sample and say, "Use this as a stylistic reference."
* **Ask for Variations:** Use prompts like "Give me three different ways to explain this concept to a student who is struggling."

### Example Prompt:
> "You are an empathetic secondary school counselor. I need to write an email to a parent about their child's sudden drop in participation. I want the tone to be supportive and collaborative, not accusatory. Suggest three bullet points for a potential improvement plan."

## ⚠️ Tool-Specific Review Checklist (Shortcomings)

* **Fact-Checking:** While Claude is less prone to "hallucination" than some models, always verify specific dates or niche scientific data.
* **Internet Access:** Standard Claude (free/Pro) does not browse the live web; it relies on its training data. Check current events manually.
* **Usage Limits:** The free tier has a daily limit on the number of messages. Plan your heavy "lesson planning sessions" accordingly.
* **Privacy Warning:** Do not upload student-identifiable data or private school records.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Mastered This Tool
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Upload a curriculum document and generate a 5-step lesson plan based on it.
* Use the "Artifacts" view to refine a structured document like a rubric or a student handout.
* Successfully adjust the "warmth" or "professionalism" of a generated email through follow-up prompts.
