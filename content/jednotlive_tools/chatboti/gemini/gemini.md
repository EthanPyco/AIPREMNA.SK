# Gemini: Standardized Guide for Teachers

---

## 1. General Use Case
Gemini is Google’s multimodal AI, designed to be deeply integrated with the tools many teachers already use daily. Its core strength lies in its ability to connect with Google Workspace (Docs, Drive, Gmail) and provide real-time information from Google Search, making it a powerful research and productivity hub.

### 🛠️ Recommended Use Cases
* **Summarizing Notes:** Quickly finding and summarizing information from your own Google Drive folders.
* **Creating Exams:** Generating questions and instantly exporting them to a Google Doc for formatting.
* **Writing Emails:** Drafting responses based on context found in your school Gmail threads.
* **Creating Cheat Sheets:** Synthesizing information from YouTube educational videos and web sources.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step UI Workflow

`[Access/Login] ➔ [Prompting/Input] ➔ [Interacting/Refining] ➔ [Finalizing/Exporting]`

### Step 1: Accessing the Tool
Navigate to gemini.google.com. Log in with your school or personal Google account. Gemini is also available as a dedicated mobile app on Android and within the Google app on iOS.

### Step 2: Providing Input
Type your request in the chat bar. You can use the `@` symbol to trigger extensions (e.g., `@Google Drive` to find a specific file). You can also upload images or use the microphone for voice-to-text input.

### Step 3: Refining the Result
Gemini provides a "Double-check response" button (the Google G icon) that uses Google Search to verify claims in the AI's answer. Use the "Modify response" icon to quickly make the answer shorter, longer, or more casual.

## ✍️ Effective Prompting for Gemini

Gemini thrives on web-connected tasks and integration-based requests.

### How to Write Prompts for this Tool:
* **Leverage the Ecosystem:** Use prompts like "Summarize the key points from my last three emails from the Principal."
* **Multimodal Search:** Upload a photo of a textbook diagram and ask, "Explain this process in simple terms for an 8th grader."
* **Source Verification:** Always ask, "Provide links to the sources used for this summary."

### Example Prompt:
> "Search my Google Drive for the PDF titled 'Biology_Syllabus_2024'. Based on that document, create a 10-question Multiple Choice quiz on 'Photosynthesis'. When finished, provide a button to export this directly to a Google Doc."

---

## ⚠️ Tool-Specific Review Checklist (Shortcomings)

* **Fact-Checking (The 'Google' Check):** Use the built-in "Double-check" button, as Gemini can occasionally prioritize fluency over factual precision.
* **Draft Variations:** Gemini often provides three "drafts" for every response. If you don't like the first one, check the others at the top of the chat.
* **Data Privacy:** Be mindful of "Workspace" settings. Ensure your school's Google Admin has enabled Gemini with appropriate data protections for staff.
* **Privacy Warning:** Do not include student names or private identifiers in your prompts.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Mastered This Tool
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Successfully generate content and export it to Google Docs or Gmail with one click.
* Use the `@Google Drive` extension to retrieve and summarize your own teaching materials.
* Use the "Double-check" feature to verify the accuracy of a generated historical or scientific explanation.
