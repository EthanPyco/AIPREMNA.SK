# AI-Powered Presentations: A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Transform raw lesson text into structured, engaging slide layouts and teacher scripts in seconds. This guide helps teachers reclaim their time by automating the tedious aspects of presentation design, allowing them to focus on active instruction and student engagement while maintaining factual accuracy.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **[Gemini](../../../jednotlive_tools/generacia_obrazkov/gemini/gemini.md) / [ChatGPT](../../../jednotlive_tools/chatboti/chatgpt/chatgpt.md) / Claude:** Excellent for generating structured outlines, slide content, and speaker notes from raw text.
* **Microsoft Copilot:** Directly integrates into PowerPoint to generate slides from prompts or existing documents.
* **Canva Magic Design:** Automatically generates visual presentation layouts from a simple text description.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Select Source Material] ➔ [Generate Outline] ➔ [Draft Visuals & Scripts] ➔ [Import/Format] ➔ [Review]`

### Step 1: Select Your Source Material
Provide the AI with your verified lesson facts, a textbook excerpt, or a curriculum outline. This ensures the presentation remains accurate and aligned with your learning objectives.

### Step 2: Generate a Structured Outline
Ask the AI to slice your material into clear, slide-ready chunks. Specify the number of slides and the target grade level to ensure appropriate complexity.

### Step 3: Draft Visual Content and Teacher Scripts
Command the AI to generate both the minimal text for the slides and the corresponding spoken script for the teacher simultaneously to ensure perfect alignment between visuals and speech.

---

## ✍️ Prompt Engineering Section

### Prompt Template:
> "Act as a professional presentation designer. Based on the provided text about [Topic], transform it into a [Number]-slide layout for [Grade Level] students. For each slide, include a 'Slide Title', [Number] bullet points, and a 'Teacher's Script'. **Rule:** Use only the facts from my text."

### Example Prompt:
> "Act as a professional presentation designer. Below is my raw text about the Water Cycle. Transform this text into a clean slide-by-slide layout for a 5-slide presentation for 6th graders. For each slide, write exactly one clear 'Slide Title' and maximum 3 short bullet points. Also, include a 3-sentence teacher script for each slide."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Visual Minimalism:** Ensure slides aren't overwhelmed by text. The AI should prioritize high-impact key points.
* **Factual Integrity:** Cross-reference the generated slides with your source material to catch any hallucinations or misinterpretations.
* **Privacy:** **Mandatory Warning:** Do not upload student-specific performance data or sensitive personal information when generating classroom presentations.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Convert a paragraph of complex text into a structured 3-slide presentation outline with speaker notes.
* Use the "Source-First" rule to ensure all generated slide content is factually accurate.
* Generate a presentation outline that is compatible with automated slide-creation tools.
* Distinguish effectively between content meant for student viewing and content meant for the teacher's spoken script.

---

## 🚀 Optional Extension: Automated Conversion
Copy the generated structured outline into an AI-powered presentation tool (like Microsoft Copilot or a dedicated AI slide app) to automatically generate the final visual slides, including layouts and images, with a single click.
