# AI-Powered Project-Based Learning (PBL): A Guide for Teachers

## Goal
Design complex, engaging, and standards-aligned projects in minutes. Use AI to brainstorm project themes, define driving questions, and create structured milestones that guide students through deep inquiry and authentic problem-solving.

### 🛠️ Recommended AI Tools
* **[ChatGPT](../../../jednotlive_tools/chatboti/chatgpt/chatgpt.md) / Claude:** Excellent for complex planning, role-play scenarios, and multi-phase project design.
* **[Gemini](../../../jednotlive_tools/generacia_obrazkov/gemini/gemini.md):** Great for cross-referencing project goals with real-world data and current events.
* **Trello / Asana:** Use AI to generate task lists that can be imported into project management tools for student teams.

---

## 🔄 The Step-by-Step Workflow

`[Identify Standards] ➔ [Brainstorm Themes] ➔ [Define Driving Question] ➔ [Create Milestones] ➔ [Review]`

### Step 1: Identify Learning Standards
Start with the curriculum goals you need to hit. Input these into the AI to ensure the project remains educationally grounded.

### Step 2: Brainstorm Authentic Themes
Ask the AI for project ideas that connect your standards to real-world problems (e.g., local environmental issues, historical mysteries, or community needs).

### Step 3: Craft the Driving Question
Use the AI to refine a "Driving Question" that is open-ended, challenging, and sparks student curiosity.

### Step 4: Map Out Milestones & Deliverables
Create a week-by-week plan with specific tasks, research phases, and final products (e.g., a podcast, a model, or a presentation).

## ✍️ Effective Prompting

### Prompt Template:
> "Act as a PBL (Project-Based Learning) specialist. Based on these standards: [Insert Standards], suggest 3 authentic project themes for [Grade Level] students. For each theme, provide a 'Driving Question' and a final 'Public Product' idea."

### Example Prompt:
> "Act as a PBL specialist. Based on 8th-grade biology standards about ecosystems and human impact, suggest 3 authentic project themes. For each, provide a Driving Question and a final product idea (like a documentary or a community proposal). Focus on local environmental challenges."

---

## ⚠️ The "Human-in-the-Loop" Review Checklist
* **Feasibility:** Can students actually complete this project with the resources available in your school/community?
* **Time Management:** Is the project timeline realistic, or is it too ambitious for the allotted class time?
* **Assessment:** Does the final product actually demonstrate mastery of the original learning standards?
* **Privacy Warning:** Do not share specific student team data or personal information in the project planning phase.

---

## ✅ How to Know You Have Understood This Guide
By the end of this guide, you should be able to:
* Generate 3 unique PBL themes aligned with specific curriculum standards.
* Create a high-quality "Driving Question" that guides a student project.
* Draft a multi-week project roadmap with clear milestones and deliverables.
